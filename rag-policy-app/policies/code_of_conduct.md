# Code of Conduct Policy

**Document ID:** HR-003  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Department:** Human Resources

---

## 1. Purpose

TechCorp Inc. is committed to maintaining a professional, respectful, and inclusive workplace. This Code of Conduct sets the standards of behavior expected from every employee.

## 2. Professional Conduct

All employees are expected to:
- Treat colleagues, clients, and partners with **dignity and respect** at all times.
- Act with **honesty and integrity** in all business dealings.
- Represent TechCorp professionally in all internal and external communications.
- Comply with all applicable laws and company policies.

## 3. Anti-Harassment and Anti-Discrimination

TechCorp has a **zero-tolerance policy** for harassment and discrimination based on:
- Race, color, national origin, or ethnicity
- Gender, gender identity, or gender expression
- Sexual orientation
- Age (40 and over)
- Disability (physical or mental)
- Religion or creed
- Pregnancy or parental status
- Veteran status

### 3.1 What Constitutes Harassment
- Verbal: offensive jokes, slurs, unwanted comments about appearance
- Physical: unwanted touching, assault
- Visual: offensive posters, images, or gestures
- Sexual harassment: unwanted sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or any sexual conduct that creates a hostile work environment

### 3.2 Reporting Harassment
- Employees who experience or witness harassment should report it immediately to their manager, HR, or via the anonymous ethics hotline: **1-800-555-ETHICS** (1-800-555-3842).
- All reports will be investigated promptly and confidentially.
- Retaliation against any person who reports harassment in good faith is strictly prohibited.

## 4. Conflict of Interest

Employees must avoid situations where their personal interests conflict with the interests of TechCorp, including:
- Holding a financial interest in a competitor or supplier
- Accepting gifts or payments from vendors beyond the allowed threshold (see Section 5)
- Hiring family members or close friends without HR disclosure
- Using company resources for personal financial gain

Employees must disclose potential conflicts of interest to HR using the **Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form**.

## 5. Gifts and Entertainment

- Employees may accept gifts from external parties valued at no more than **$50**.
- Gifts exceeding $50 must be reported to and approved by HR.
- Employees may not accept cash or cash-equivalent gifts (gift cards) of any amount.
- Offering gifts to government officials is strictly prohibited.

## 6. Use of Company Resources

- Company equipment, software, and networks are for business use. **Limited personal use is permitted** provided it does not interfere with job duties.
- Company resources must not be used for any illegal activity.
- Employees must not use company time, equipment, or resources to run a personal business.

## 7. Confidentiality

- Employees must protect the confidentiality of TechCorp's proprietary information, trade secrets, and customer data.
- This obligation continues after employment ends.
- Employees must sign and comply with the company's Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA).

## 8. Social Media Policy

- Employees may identify themselves as TechCorp employees on personal social media but must make clear that their views are their own.
- Employees must not post confidential company information, financial data, or disparaging content about TechCorp, colleagues, or clients.
- Employees representing TechCorp on official social media channels must obtain approval from the Marketing team.

## 9. Disciplinary Actions

Violations of the Code of Conduct may result in:
1. Verbal warning
2. Written warning
3. Suspension (paid or unpaid)
4. Termination of employment

Serious violations (e.g., harassment, fraud, violence) may result in immediate termination without prior warning.

## 10. Reporting Violations

Employees can report Code of Conduct violations via:
- Direct conversation with their manager or HR
- Email: ethics@techcorp.com
- Anonymous ethics hotline: 1-800-555-ETHICS

All reports are taken seriously and investigated impartially.
