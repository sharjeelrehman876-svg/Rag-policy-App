# Employee Onboarding Policy

**Document ID:** HR-006  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Department:** Human Resources

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy ensures that all new employees at TechCorp Inc. receive a consistent, welcoming, and effective onboarding experience that enables them to become productive members of the organization quickly.

## 2. Pre-Boarding (Before Day 1)

HR will initiate the following before the employee's first day:
- Send a welcome email with first-day logistics (start time, location/remote link, dress code)
- Provide access to the **New Hire Portal** to complete paperwork digitally (tax forms, direct deposit, benefits enrollment)
- Ship or prepare equipment (laptop, badge, peripherals)
- Assign a **buddy** from the employee's team for the first 30 days
- Set up system accounts (email, Slack, HR portal) to be ready on Day 1

Benefits enrollment must be completed within **30 days** of start date. Failure to enroll within this window results in the employee being defaulted to the basic health plan with no dental or vision coverage until the next open enrollment period.

## 3. Day 1 Schedule

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 9:00 AM | Welcome meeting with HR |
| 9:30 AM | IT setup and systems access |
| 10:30 AM | Company overview presentation |
| 12:00 PM | Welcome lunch with team |
| 1:00 PM | Meet with direct manager |
| 2:00 PM | Review 30/60/90-day plan |
| 3:00 PM | Tour (or virtual introduction for remote employees) |

## 4. First 30 Days

- Complete all mandatory compliance training (Security Awareness, Code of Conduct, Anti-Harassment) within the first **30 days**.
- Schedule 1:1 meetings with key stakeholders and cross-functional partners.
- Shadow team members to understand workflows and tools.
- Complete the 30-day check-in with HR to address any early concerns.

## 5. First 60 Days

- Begin contributing independently to projects with manager support.
- Attend all relevant team meetings.
- Complete the 60-day check-in with direct manager.
- Finalize 90-day goal documentation in the HR portal.

## 6. First 90 Days (Probationary Period End)

- Formal **90-day performance review** conducted by direct manager (see HR-004).
- Successful completion of the probationary period unlocks eligibility for:
  - PTO usage
  - Remote work requests
  - Access to the Employee Development budget ($500/year for training)
- Employees who do not meet expectations at the 90-day mark may be placed on a PIP or have employment terminated.

## 7. Buddy Program

- Every new hire is paired with an experienced employee (the "buddy") in the same or adjacent team.
- Buddy responsibilities: answer informal questions, introduce the new hire to team norms, check in weekly for the first 30 days.
- Buddy assignments are voluntary and are recognized in the quarterly employee shout-out program.

## 8. Mandatory Training (First 30 Days)

| Training Module | Completion Deadline |
|---|---|
| Security Awareness (IT-001) | Day 30 |
| Code of Conduct (HR-003) | Day 30 |
| Anti-Harassment Training | Day 30 |
| Data Privacy & GDPR Basics | Day 30 |
| Expense Policy Overview (FIN-001) | Day 30 |

Failure to complete mandatory training by the deadline will result in a system access restriction until training is completed.
