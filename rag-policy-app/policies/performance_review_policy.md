# Performance Review Policy

**Document ID:** HR-004  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Department:** Human Resources

---

## 1. Overview

TechCorp Inc. is committed to supporting the growth and development of all employees through a structured, fair, and transparent performance review process.

## 2. Review Cycle

- **Annual Review:** Conducted once per year in **December** for all employees. Results determine merit increases effective January 1.
- **Mid-Year Check-In:** A non-evaluative conversation in **June** between employee and manager to assess progress on goals.
- **90-Day Review:** All new employees receive a formal review at the end of their 90-day probationary period.
- **Probationary Period:** Employees who do not meet expectations during the 90-day review may be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) or have their employment terminated.

## 3. Performance Rating Scale

| Rating | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Exceptional | Consistently exceeds all expectations; role model for peers |
| 4 | Exceeds Expectations | Regularly surpasses most goals |
| 3 | Meets Expectations | Achieves all core goals; solid contributor |
| 2 | Partially Meets Expectations | Meets some but not all goals; improvement needed |
| 1 | Does Not Meet Expectations | Fails to meet most goals; immediate action required |

## 4. Salary Increases Based on Performance

| Rating | Merit Increase |
|---|---|
| Exceptional (5) | 6–8% |
| Exceeds Expectations (4) | 4–5% |
| Meets Expectations (3) | 2–3% |
| Partially Meets (2) | 0–1% |
| Does Not Meet (1) | 0% (PIP required) |

- Merit increases are subject to budget availability and are not guaranteed.
- Employees on an active PIP are not eligible for merit increases.

## 5. Goal Setting

- At the start of each year (January), employees set **3–5 SMART goals** in collaboration with their manager.
- Goals must be documented in the HR performance portal.
- Goals may be adjusted mid-year during the June check-in with manager approval.

## 6. Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

A PIP is initiated when an employee receives a rating of 1 (Does Not Meet Expectations) or has persistent performance issues.

- PIP duration: **30 to 90 days**, depending on the severity of the issues.
- The PIP will outline specific, measurable improvement targets.
- The employee will have **weekly check-ins** with their manager during the PIP period.
- Employees who successfully complete a PIP are removed from probation; those who do not may be terminated.
- Employees on a PIP are not eligible for remote work or promotions.

## 7. Promotion Process

- Promotions are considered during the **annual review cycle**.
- Employees must have a rating of **Exceeds Expectations (4) or higher** for at least one consecutive year.
- Promotions are subject to role availability and budget approval.
- Employees promoted mid-year receive a salary adjustment at the time of promotion and are not eligible for an additional merit increase in that same year.

## 8. Appeals Process

Employees who disagree with their performance rating may file a formal appeal within **15 business days** of receiving the review:
1. Submit a written appeal to HR (hr@techcorp.com).
2. HR will review the appeal and consult with the manager's supervisor.
3. A decision will be returned within **10 business days** of receiving the appeal.

## 9. Confidentiality

Performance reviews are strictly confidential. Managers must not disclose an employee's rating to other employees. Violations of this confidentiality are a Code of Conduct violation.

## 10. Contact

For performance review questions, contact hr@techcorp.com or extension 1100.
